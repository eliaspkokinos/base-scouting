Role archetypes tracked
Role definition
Anchoring #6
A holding midfielder who sits deep, screens the backline, and recycles possession with calm, positional intelligence. The team's defensive foundation in midfield — not glamorous, but quietly essential to everything else working.
Similar archetypes
Claude Makélélé, Florentino, Mohamed Camara
Role definition
Hybrid #6
A modern pivot capable of anchoring the midfield defensively while also carrying or driving forward into more advanced zones. The most coveted defensive midfield profile right now — because they do both jobs without compromising either.
Similar archetypes
Rodri, Declan Rice, Moisés Caicedo
Role definition
Ball-Winning Midfielder
Defensive-minded midfielders who hunt second balls, thrive in duels, and add intensity through relentless pressing. Their value is measured in disruption — they make the opposition's build-up uncomfortable before it even gets started.
Similar archetypes
Gavi, Eduardo Camavinga, Casemiro
Role definition
Box-to-Box Midfielder
Relentless, end-to-end runners who impact all phases — breaking up opposition attacks and arriving late in the box to contribute offensively. They cover more ground than almost anyone on the pitch and make it look sustainable.
Similar archetypes
Frank Lampard, Steven Gerrard, Bastian Schweinsteiger
Role definition
Box-Crashing Midfielder
Aggressive midfield runners who time their surges into the penalty area to finish moves and stretch defences. The threat isn't in their build-up play — it's in the run they make when the ball is on the other side of the pitch.
Similar archetypes
Mikel Merino, Scott McTominay, Jude Bellingham
Role definition
Roaming Midfielder
Creative, unshackled midfielders who drift between lines, link play, and break through defensive blocks with movement and flair. They don't have a fixed zone — their job is to find space, and they're very good at finding it.
Similar archetypes
Paul Pogba, Kevin De Bruyne, Pedri
Role definition
Deep-Lying Playmaker
A tempo-setter who operates from deep, controlling transitions and distributing with vision and range under pressure. They run the game without the ball going through them constantly — and the best ones make the tempo invisible.
Similar archetypes
Andrea Pirlo, Sergio Busquets, Xabi Alonso
Role definition
Advanced Playmaker
Technically gifted creators who operate high between the lines, threading incisive passes and orchestrating final third actions. They live in the space between midfield and attack — and in that space, they're some of the most dangerous players on the pitch.
Similar archetypes
Kaká, Michel Platini, Mesut Özil
Role definition
Wide Playmaker
Inverted wide players who drift centrally to influence possession, offering combination play and incisive passing over traditional wing play. The difference from an inside forward is intent — they're looking to create, not score.
Similar archetypes
Bernardo Silva, Cole Palmer, Riyad Mahrez
