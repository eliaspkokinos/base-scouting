Observed Behaviors

On the Ball

  • Scans before receiving, decides before contact. Head-up frequency during buildup is already a formed habit. The technical speed this enables — playing quickly in tight spaces without appearing rushed — is the foundation of everything else.

  • Passes with intent, not just accuracy. Distribution rarely circulates for its own sake. Each ball either breaks a line, changes the angle of attack, or releases a runner. 100th-percentile long pass accuracy reflects quality and selectivity in combination.

  • Secure first touch in tight central areas. Absorbs contact using balance and body positioning. Rarely loses the ball to a bad touch on the receive — the technique is already reliable, not developing.

  • Finishes with conviction from range. 97th-percentile npxG per shot reflects good selection as much as technique. He attempts the strikes worth attempting and converts them at an elite rate.

  • Two-footed without telegraphing the switch. Uses both feet in combination play and set-piece delivery. The choice of foot does not signal the direction of the pass, which is what makes the two-footedness genuinely dangerous rather than merely functional.

Off the Ball

  • Finds space between the lines before the ball arrives. Positions off the shoulder of opposing midfielders to receive on the half-turn. The habit of staying off defensive shoulders is consistent across match states.

  • Times late arrivals into the box precisely. 98th-percentile box touches and 18 G/A from 23 starts reflect arrival timing as much as positional sense. Arrives when the ball is ready, not before — which means arriving unmarked more often than expected.

  • Directs teammates on the pitch. Vocal in buildup, points to space. A natural leadership pattern at 17, as one of the youngest players in a senior squad.

  • Reactive in the defensive phase. Intercepts angles rather than hunting duels. 5th percentile in defensive actions. Not a defensive midfielder, and the energy management is deliberate — he conserves output to be available for the moments that matter.

Development

Priorities for Growth

01

Build the progressive passing habit, not just the quality. Long pass accuracy at the 100th percentile. The frequency of progressive attempts at the 42nd. The gap between what he is capable of and what he defaults to is the most important developmental question — and it requires a system that rewards the attempt before the habit becomes consistent.

02

Physical duel engagement. Duels won at the 10th percentile. Partly a habits question — he does not currently seek contact — and partly a physical development question. Deliberate work on body use in close areas, combined with natural growth over two to three years, is the practical route. A double-pivot partner is the structural near-term solution.

03

Expand the defensive action floor. He is not going to become a defensive midfielder. But modest improvement in defensive volume would meaningfully widen the range of systems he can operate in. A calibration task, not a profile change.

04

Accumulate experience against physically mature opponents. The technical base is there. The sequencing matters. A developmental environment before top-five league exposure is the responsible route and the one most likely to protect what is already exceptional.

The technical and cognitive qualities that make him exceptional are not system-dependent. Press resistance, two-foot distribution, scanning habits, clutch goalscoring — these will show up in any possession-based environment that gives him the ball in central areas. That is the basis for the score.

The discount is honest rather than cautious. Physical maturity against older, stronger midfielders is still an open question. The defensive contribution floor is low enough that system matching matters more for him than for most players at this age.

Think: possession-based, technically demanding, double-pivot structure behind him. La Liga and Eredivisie are the natural fits. A transition-heavy system that asks him to win individual duels acquires the wrong version of this player and may not develop the right one.

Scout's Read

The data does not leave much ambiguity on the output side. Non-penalty goals per 90 and npxG per 90 both at the 100th percentile among all 60 Serbian SuperLiga midfielders. Assists per 90 at the 98th. Touches in box per 90 at the 98th. Shot assists at the 95th. At 17, in 23 starts for Red Star Belgrade. These are not soft percentiles inflated by a low comparison pool — V. Natcho, T. Elšnik, and R. Krunić are in that pool. He is ahead of all of them on attacking output.

The 42nd-percentile progressive passes figure is the most interesting number in the whole profile, precisely because everything else suggests it should be higher. Long pass accuracy at the 100th percentile. Decision quality high. What is low is the frequency — he attempts the progressive ball less often than his quality would suggest is optimal. Some of that is system; some is positional role; some may be a habit still forming. That gap is the most important developmental question.

The market has not priced this correctly. €3.5M for the 17-year-old who leads all SuperLiga midfielders in four attacking categories simultaneously, with no injury history, called into the senior national squad. A possession-based club that acquires him now, builds the right double-pivot partner alongside him, and operates a system that rewards progressive attempts is buying a future top-five league starter at a price that will look different in 18 months.

Final assessment

Verdict & Potential Rating

Scout's Verdict

A 17-year-old advanced playmaker who leads all Serbian SuperLiga midfielders in four attacking output categories simultaneously, with no injury history, a senior national team call-up, and a leadership profile that does not usually arrive at this age. The defensive contribution is minimal and system matching matters because of it. The progressive passing habit is developing rather than settled. Both are honest limitations in a profile that is otherwise among the strongest seen at this age in the Balkan region.

What travels

  • Technical press resistance in tight central areas — a formed habit, not a developing tendency
  • Two-foot distribution quality, including 100th-percentile long pass accuracy without telegraphing foot choice
  • Clutch goalscoring — 100th percentile np goals per 90, decisive in high-stakes matches
  • Pre-reception scanning and spatial positioning — already a cognitive habit at 17
  • Leadership pattern and competitive composure against senior opposition

What must be addressed

  • Progressive passing frequency — the quality exists; the default habit to attempt it does not yet
  • Duel engagement — 10th percentile; a physical double-pivot partner is a system requirement
  • Defensive contribution floor — 5th percentile in defensive actions limits viable system types
  • Physical development against mature, stronger midfield opponents at the next level
